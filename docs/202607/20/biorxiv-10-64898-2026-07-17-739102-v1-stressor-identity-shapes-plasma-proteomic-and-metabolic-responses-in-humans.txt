Abstract 71 

Acute stress triggers rapid physiological adaptations, yet how distinct stress modalities shape the 72 

human circulating molecular landscape remains unclear. We performed longitudinal deep plasma 73 

proteomics and targeted metabolomics across three paradigms: psychological stress, controlled 74 

physical stress, and combined psychological –physical stress induced by bung ee jumping. Despite 75 

robust hypothalamic –pituitary –adrenal axis activation, psychological stress induced minimal 76 

plasma proteomic and metabolic changes, whereas physical stress triggered rapid, coordinated 77 

proteome remodeling accompanied by stressor -specifi c metabolic adaptations. Combined stress 78 

elicited the strongest and most persistent proteomic response, defining a graded molecular 79 

signature across stress modalities. Cross -paradigm integration identified a stress -associated 80 

protein core enriched for immu ne granule effectors, RNA -binding proteins, and vesicle -linked 81 

signatures, suggesting regulated extracellular release. Consistently, extracellular vesicle profiling 82 

in the mixed -stress paradigm supported vesicle -associated secretion, while functional exper iments 83 

indicated that glucocorticoid signaling alone is insufficient to trigger release. Together, these 84 

findings reveal stressor identity as a key determinant of the human circulating molecular response. 85

Introduction 94 

The acute stress response is a fundamental physiological adaptive process that enables organisms to 95 

cope with environmental challenges. Triggered by perceived threats ranging from psychological and 96 

social stressors to intense physical demands, acute stress rapidly mobilizes neuroendocrine, metabolic, 97 

and immune systems to suppo rt adaptive behavioral and physiological responses (Chrousos & Gold, 98 

1992 ). Under healthy conditions, these responses are tightly regulated and resolved once the threat 99 

subsides, allowing restoration of homeostasis. In contrast, prolonged or dysregulated s tress responses 100 

contribute to the development of numerous pathological conditions, including cardiovascular disease, 101 

metabolic dysfunction, and psychiatric disorders (de Kloet & Joëls, 2023; McEwen et al., 1998 ). 102 

Historically, stress research has focused on central nervous system circuits and activation of the 103 

hypothalamic –pituitary –adrenal (HPA) axis . However, growing evidence indicates that acute stress 104 

rapidly reshapes the circulating molecular environment, reflecting coordinated responses across multip le 105 

organs and physiological systems (Rohleder et al., 2019; Galluzzi et al., 2018 ). Plasma serves as a central 106 

integrative compartment for systemic signaling , containing endocrine mediators , immune -derived 107 

proteins, metabolic intermediates , and cell -derive d vesicles that together mediate the inter -organ 108 

communication. Advances in high -resolution omics technologies have begun to reveal the complexity 109 

of these circulating responses. Plasma proteomics and metabolomics studies have identified stress -110 

associated changes in inflammatory mediators, metabolic pathways, and acute -phase proteins in humans 111 

exposed to psychological or physiological challenges (Marsland et al., 2017; Steptoe et al. , 2007; 112 

Contrepois et al., 2020 ). Despite these advances, a central questio n remains unresolved: do different 113 

forms of acute stress (e.g. physical versus social -emotional stress) engage shared systemic molecular 114 

programs, or do they elicit fundamentally distinct circulating response patterns? 115 

This question has important implicati ons for ad vancing our understanding of stress -related 116 

pathophysiology. Psychological and physical stressors differ fundamentally in their cognitive appraisal, 117 

metabolic demands, mechanical load and in their valence: whereas exercise represents a predominan tly 118 

positive, adaptive stressor (eustress), psychological stress is frequently experienced as negative and 119 

uncontrollable (distress), and is strongly linked to adverse health outcomes when chronic or dysregulated 120 

(Heidt et al., 2014; Knezevic et al ., 2023; Herhaus et al., 2024; Gaab et al., 2005 ). Despite this 121 

fundamental difference in valence, both modalities reliably activate canonical neuroendocrine pathways, 122 

particularly the HPA axis. Determining whether these stress modalities converge on common system ic 123 

pathways or instead trigger modality -specific circulating responses is essential for understanding how 124 

stress signals propagate th roughout the organism. Beyond identifying stress -responsive molecules, an 125 

additional open question concerns the mechanisms by which intracellular stress signals are transmitted 126 

into the circulation. Increasing evidence suggests that non -classical secretion pathways, including 127 

extracellular vesicle (EV) release and unconventional protein secretion, play key roles in intercellul ar 128 

stress communication. EVs can transport proteins, RNA -binding factors, and damage -associated 129 

molecular patterns (DAMPs) between tissues, thereby propagating stress signals systemically ( Tkach 130

Here, we performed longitudinal deep plasma proteomics and targeted metabolomics across three 133 

complementary human stress paradigms: purely psychological stress induced by the Trier Social Stress 134 

Test (TSST), controlled physical stress induced by maximal exercise on a bicycle ergometer , and mixed 135 

psychological –physical stress induced by bungee jumping. These paradigms span a graded spectrum o f136 

stress exposure , differing in cognitive appraisal, physiological load, and emotional salience while 137 

reliably eliciting acute stress responses. Using nanoparticle -enabled plasma proteomics ( Blume et al., 138 

2020 ), together with targeted metabolomics, we quan tified thousands of circulating molecules a t139 

multiple timepoints to resolve stressor -specific molecular trajectories. Across these paradigms, we 140 

identify a stress -associated protein core that emerges under conditions of intense physiological stress 141 

and bec omes amplified during combined psychological –physical challenge. Integrating EV -profiling 142 

with cellular and in vivo perturbation experiments, we further implicate EV -mediated unconventional 143 

secretion pathways as a central mechanism linking intracellular stress states to systemic plasma 144 

remodeling. 145

Results 147 

Psychological stress induces robust endocrine activation but minimal plasma proteomic changes 148 

To define how distinct stress modalities shape systemic molecular responses in human s, we first 149 

examined changes in plasma proteins in response to purely psychological stress using plasma samples 150 

collected during the TSST in a cohort comprising 20 healthy controls and 20 individuals diagnosed with 151 

panic disorder (Table S1) . The TSST is a highly standardized and widely used laboratory paradigm that 152 

robustly induces acute psychosocial stress through socially evaluative public speaking and mental 153 

arithmetic ( Kirschbaum et al., 1993 ). Plasma samples were obtained at multiple timepoints before and 154 

after stress exposure, enabling longitudinal assessment of endocrine, proteomic, and metabolomic 155 

response ( Fig. 1A, B). Serum cortisol measurements confirmed robust activation of the HPA axis across 156 

participants, with cortisol levels peaking shortly af ter stress induction and gradually returning toward 157 

baseline, without differences between patients with panic disorder and healthy controls ( Fig. 1 C). 158 

To enable sensitive detection of stress -induced changes in the circulating proteome, we implemented a 159 

nov el deep plasma proteomics approach utilizing surface -functionalized superparamagnetic 160 

nanoparticles (NPs) to fractionate plasma proteins (Fig. 1B) . This optimized workflow markedly 161 

enhanced our detection capability, enabling quantification of > 6000 prote ins per sample after filtering 162 

across the TSST dataset (Fig. S1A -F) . Each sample was fractionated with two types of nanoparticles 163 

(NP1/2, Fig. 1B) and analyzed separately via LC -MS, providing high -resolution insights into plasma 164 

protein abundance. 165 

Despite this extensive proteomic depth, differential abundance analysis comparing post -stress time 166 

points with the immediate pre -stress baseline revealed only a few significantly regulated proteins 167

Mixed psychological –physical stress induces amplified and sustained plasma proteome remodeling 244 

Having defined systemic molecular responses to isolate d psychological and physical stress, we next 245 

investigated a paradigm combining both modalities. We analyzed plasma samples from 22 male 246 

participants undergoing bungee jump ing , a naturalistic paradigm combining acute psychological threat 247 

with intense physic al load ( Fig. 3 A; Table S12 ). Blood samples were collected at baseline ( 7 days befo re 248 

the bungee jump), 50 and 15 minutes prior to the jump to capture anticipatory stress responses and at 249 

multiple timepoints following the jump (20, 60, 120 and 240 minutes) , as well as at a late follow -up 7 -250 

10 days later. This longitudinal sampling scheme enabl ed assessment of both anticipatory and post -251 

stress molecular dynamics across acute stress and recovery phases. Moreover, this setting enabled us to 252 

test whether simult aneous exposure to intense psychological threat and high mechanical load amplifies 253 

systemic molecular responses beyond those observed for either stress modality alone. 254 

Initially, we assessed whether our setup induced psychological stress in the participant s. Self -report 255 

questionnaire responses indicated an increase in subjective stress, arousal, and enthusiasm among the 256 

jumpers, which declined by the end of the experiment (Fig. S5A). Critically, these subjective responses 257 

were mirrored at the endocrine leve l: plasma cortisol measurements confirmed a significant increase 258 

immediately after the jump, followed by a subsequent decline back toward baseline (Fig. 3B) , providing 259 

converging evidence that bungee jumping reliably engaged both the psychological and phys iological 260 

stress axes. 261 

To determine whether the endocrine activation observed during bungee jumping translated into systemic 262 

proteomic changes, we next profiled the circulating plasma proteome across the experiment (Table S13) .263 

In contrast to the largely stable plasma proteome observed during purely psychological stress and the 264

following bungee jumping, with widespread regulation that peak ed 4 h post -jump ( Fig. 3C; Table S1 4). 267

Adaptive responses to acute stress require the rapid integration of cognitive appraisal, endocrine 427 

signaling, metabolic adaptation, and immune activation. By combining deep plasma proteomics and 428 

targeted metabolomics across three complementary human stress paradigms, psychological stress 429 

(TSST), physical exertion , and mixed psychological -physical stress, our study reveals that the 430 

qualitative nature of a stressor critically determines whether systemic molecular remodeling occurs. 431 

These data define a gradien t of circulating responses in which endocrine activation alone is insufficient 432 

to drive large -scale plasma proteome remodeling, whereas stressors imposing metabolic and mechanical 433 

load engage coordinated systemic secretory programs. 434 

435 

A striking observation is the dissociation between hormonal stress activation and circulating proteomic 436 

responses. Despite robust HPA axis activation, purely psychological stress induced by the TSST 437 

resulted in minimal plasma proteomic remodeling. This finding challenges the co mmon assumption 438 

that glucocorticoid signaling alone orchestrates systemic stress responses and instead suggests that 439 

endocrine activation is dissociable from large -scale protein release into circulation, pointing to 440 

additional physiological inputs, such as metabolic or mechanical load, as critical drivers of systemic 441 

secretory responses. Notably, this compartmentalization was also evident in individuals with panic 442 

disorder, a population with heightened stress sensitivity ( Abelson et al., 2007 ), but showed n o443 

amplified peripheral proteomic responses. This stability of the plasma proteome likely reflects strong 444 

systemic buffering of transient neuroendocrine stress signals. The few regulated proteins were 445 

predominantly intracellular stress - and cytoskeleton -ass ociated factors, suggesting selective release 446 

from activated immune or vascular cells rather than widespread secretory responses. Together, these 447 

observations suggest psychological stress alone largely remains confined to neuroendocrine signaling, 448 

while sy stemic plasma remodeling requires more intense or traumatic experiences or additional 449 

physiological inputs. In contrast, both physical exercise and mixed psychological –physical stress 450 

triggered extensive plasma proteomic responses . Across these paradigms , we identified a conserved 451 

stress -associated protein core comprising intracellular stress sensors, immune granule components, 452 

and cytoskeletal regulators. Many proteins within this core, including HMGB family members, S100 453 

proteins and neutrophil granule enzymes, belong to the class of leaderless proteins that lack classical 454 

signal peptides, suggesting secretion through unconventional pathways rather than canonical ER -Golgi 455 

route (Rammes et al., 1997; Gardella et al., 2002; Rabouille, 2017; Galluzzi et al. , 2018) . In addition, 456 

the enrichment of alarmins and neutrophil granule proteins raises the possibility that regulated cell -457 

death -associated processes, such as pyroptosis, necroptosis or NETosis, contribute to their appearance 458 

in plasma. Many of these mole cules function as damage -associated molecular patterns (DAMPs), 459 

mediating systemic danger signaling and immune activation during cellular stress and tissue strain. 460 

Our integrated extracellular vesicle (EV) analyses provide convergent evidence supporting EV-461 

mediated secretion as a major contributor to these circulating signatures. We observed a robust 462

cleared within 90 minutes ( Brahmer et al., 2019 ). 468 

The observed increase in plasma LC3B levels in the bungee jump cohort further suggests engagement 469 

of LC3 -dependent vesicle loa ding and secretion (LDELS), an autophagy -related pathway that 470 

selectively incorporat es RNA -binding and cytosolic proteins into EVs (Leidal et al., 2020) . This 471 

mechanism provides a plausible explanation for the prominent enrichment of RNA -binding proteins a nd 472 

nuclear factors within the stress -regulated plasma proteome. Notably , several strongly regulated proteins 473 

in our dataset, including HMGB1 and MMP9, have previously been shown t o undergo secretion through 474 

secretory autophagy pathways under inflammatory s tress conditions (Martinelli et al., 2021; Hisaoka -475

Beyond shared mechanisms, our data reveal pronounced temporal differences between stress modalities 478 

and there by potentially diverging stress intensities . Physical exercise induced rapid plasma proteomic 479 

changes that largely resolved during the recovery phase, consistent with the transient metabolic demands 480 

of acute exertion. In contrast, mixed psychological –physical stress elicited responses of greater 481 

amplitude and persistence, with several proteins remaining elevated for several hours after exposure .482 

The presence of anticipatory molecular changes prior to the jump demonstrate that perceived t hreat 483 

alone can initiate early systemic reorganization before the physical challenge occurs. Such anticipatory 484 

responses have been described at the endocrine and neural level but have not been observed in 485 

circulating molecular profiles (Engert et al., 2013 ; Herman et al., 2016 ). This sustained elevation of 486 

alarmins and immune -associated proteins may have important physiological implications for long -term 487 

health . Prolonged circulation of DAMP molecules such as HMGB proteins and S100 family members 488 

has been i mplicated in neuroimmune communication and systemic inflammatory priming (Andersson 489 

et al., 2018; Yang et al., 2020 ). Such mechanisms could contribute to the link between repeated stress 490 

exp osure and long -term vulnerability to cardiovascular, metabolic, an d psychiatric disorders ( Vaccarino 491 

& Bremner, 2024; Kivimäki et al., 2023; Slavich & Irwin, 2014 ). 492 

Targeted metabolomics provided independent support for the stressor -specific molecular hierarchy 493 

observed at the proteome level. Physical stress paradigms in duced broad and coordinated metabolic 494 

remodeling, with many measured metabolites significantly altered in both the exercise and bungee jump 495 

cohorts, whereas psychological stress alone elicited minimal metabolic changes in healthy individuals. 496 

This converge nce between proteomic and metabolic responses reinforces the notion that systemic 497 

molecular remodeling requires physiological inputs beyond endocrine activation alone. Notably, the 498

Our mechanistic perturbation experiments indicate that no single mediator is sufficient to re produce the 506 

observed secretory response. Neither stimulation of immune cells with cortisol, lactate, or Lac -Phe, nor 507 

acute restraint st ress in mice, even in the context of pharmacological glucocorticoid receptor 508 

degradation, recapitulate the extensive plasma remodeling observed in h umans during physical or mixed 509 

stress . These findings suggest that systemic stress secretion requires integr ation of multiple converging 510 

physiological signals, including mechanical strain, metabolic flux, and endocrine signaling, which are 511 

most strongly engaged under conditions combin ing psychological and physical challenge. 512 

Several limitations warrant consideration . First, the absence of a crossover design precluded within -513 

individual comparisons across stress paradigms, which would allow more precise dissection of stressor -514 

specific molecular effects. Second, the cellular origins of stress -regulated plas ma proteins remain 515 

incompletely resolved. Although EV signatures suggest contributions from immune and potentially 516

were represented in the TSST experiment, whereas the other two paradigms included only male 519 

participants, which may influence the generalizability of the findings. 520 

In summary, our resul ts demonstrate that acute stress is encoded in the circulating molecular landscape 521 

in a modality -dependent manner. While psychological stress remains largely confined to neuroendocrine 522 

signaling, physical and mixed stressors activate coordinated systemic p rograms characterized by EV -523 

associated unconventional protein secretion and the release of a conserved stress -associated protein core. 524 

These findings establish a framework for understanding how diverse environmental challenges shape 525 

our circulating molecu lar landscape and highlight extracellular vesicle -mediated secretion as a central 526 

mechanism linking cellular stress states to systemic communication. 527

Material and Methods 923 

924 

Human studies 925 

926 

Cohort 1: Trier Social Stress Test 927 

Ethical approval and study participants 928 

Participants were recruited through newspaper advertisements, online announcements, and notice 929 

boards at various universities. Prior to enrollment, eligibility was assessed via a structured interview 930 

based on the Str uctured Clinical Interview for Axes I and II for DSM -IV (Wittchen et al., 1997; APA, 931 

2000);. The experimental group consisted of individuals diagnosed with panic disorder according to 932 

DSM -IV criteria, aged between 18 and 65 years. A matched control group o f healthy individuals was 933 

established, paired by age and gender to the experimental group. Groups did not differ significantly in 934 

age, gender, or use of oral contraceptives (all p > 0.05). Exclusion criteria included any acute or chronic 935 

comorbid medical illness, additional psychiatric diagnoses, current psychotropic medication or 936 

substance use, known allergies, and stressful life events within the preceding 6 months. Each group 937 

comprised n = 32 participants. All participants provided written informed conse nt. The study protocol 938 

was approved by the Ethics Committee of the Landesärztekammer Rheinland -Pfalz, Germany 939 

(No#2019 -14188). 940 

941 

General study design 942 

We conducted a single -center, non -blinded, non -randomized, controlled repeated -measures prospective 943 

study t o investigate molecular and psychological responses to acute psychosocial stress in healthy 944 

controls and in panic disorder patients. All assessments were performed on weekdays between 2:00 and 945 

6:00 p.m. The participants were instructed not to eat and not d rink caffeinated drinks for at least three 946 

hours before the test session as well as to refrain from intense physical exercise and alcohol consumption 947 

for 12 h before the experiment. Female participants completed the stress test during the luteal phase of 948 

their menstrual cycle to control for hormonal influences. The study protocol lasted approximately 90 949 

min and comprised three sequential phases: a pre -stress phase, a stress phase, and a post -stress phase. 950 

951 

Psychosocial Stress Protocol 952 

The stress condition was the Trier Social Stress Test (TSST), following the Kirschbaum et al. (1993 )953 

protocol. It consisted of three sections: preparation, interview, and a calculation task, with each section 954 

lasting 5 minutes. 955
