## 摘要
背景：运动性疲劳是一种复杂的生理现象，涉及氧化应激、炎症和代谢紊乱。麦角硫因（EGT）是一种具有强效抗氧化特性的天然氨基酸，因其潜在的健康益处而备受关注。本研究旨在评估基因III(R) EGT在力竭运动小鼠模型中的抗疲劳效果，并阐明其潜在机制。

方法：雄性C57BL/6小鼠随机分为五组：对照组（CTL）、低剂量EGT组（EGT-L，10 mg/kg）、中剂量EGT组（EGT-M，30 mg/kg）、高剂量EGT组（EGT-H，50 mg/kg）和阳性对照组（辅酶Q10，50 mg/kg）。小鼠接受为期四周的跑台训练方案，随后进行力竭跑动测试。我们测量了运动表现，并在多个时间点采集血液和骨骼肌样本，以评估生化标志物、炎症细胞因子、抗氧化状态及关键信号蛋白。

结果：补充基因III(R) EGT，尤其是中、高剂量组，显著延长了力竭时间和跑动距离。与对照组相比，EGT处理显著降低了运动后乳酸（LA）、乳酸脱氢酶（LDH）和血尿素氮（BUN）水平。此外，基因III(R) EGT抑制了运动诱导的促炎细胞因子（包括IL-1β、IL-6和TNF-α）的增加。EGT的抗疲劳作用还与丙二醛（MDA）降低以及超氧化物歧化酶（SOD）和谷胱甘肽过氧化物酶（GSH-Px）活性升高有关。机制上，EGT促进了骨骼肌中AMP活化蛋白激酶（AMPK）的磷酸化和过氧化物酶体增殖物激活受体γ辅激活因子1α（PGC-1α）的表达，同时提高了Bcl-2/Bax比值，表明线粒体生物合成增强和凋亡减少。

结论：我们的研究结果表明，基因III(R) EGT能有效增强运动表现并缓解疲劳。其潜在机制涉及减轻氧化应激和炎症，以及激活AMPK/PGC-1α信号通路以促进线粒体功能和细胞保护。这些结果凸显了基因III(R) EGT作为对抗运动性疲劳的营养补充剂的潜力。

## Abstract
BackgroundExercise-induced fatigue is a complex physiological phenomenon involving oxidative stress, inflammation, and metabolic disturbances. Ergothioneine (EGT), a naturally occurring amino acid with potent antioxidant properties, has garnered interest for its potential health benefits. This study aimed to evaluate the anti-fatigue effects of Gene III(R) EGT in a mouse model of exhaustive exercise and to elucidate its underlying mechanisms.

MethodsMale C57BL/6 mice were randomly divided into five groups: a control group (CTL), low-dose EGT (EGT-L, 10 mg/kg), medium-dose EGT (EGT-M, 30 mg/kg), high-dose EGT (EGT-H, 50 mg/kg), and a positive control group (Coenzyme Q10, 50 mg/kg). Mice were subjected to a 4-week treadmill training protocol, followed by an exhaustive running test. We measured exercise performance and collected blood and skeletal muscle samples at multiple time points to assess biochemical markers, inflammatory cytokines, antioxidant status, and key signaling proteins.

ResultsGene III(R) EGT supplementation, particularly at medium and high doses, significantly extended the time to exhaustion and running distance. Compared to the control group, EGT treatment significantly reduced post-exercise levels of lactic acid (LA), lactate dehydrogenase (LDH), and blood urea nitrogen (BUN). Furthermore, Gene III(R) EGT suppressed the exercise-induced increase in pro-inflammatory cytokines, including IL-1{beta}, IL-6, and TNF-. The anti-fatigue effect of EGT was also associated with a reduction in malondialdehyde (MDA) and an increase in the activities of superoxide dismutase (SOD) and glutathione peroxidase (GSH-Px). Mechanistically, EGT promoted the phosphorylation of AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK) and the expression of peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor-gamma coactivator-1 alpha (PGC-1) in skeletal muscle, while also increasing the Bcl-2/Bax ratio, suggesting enhanced mitochondrial biogenesis and reduced apoptosis.

ConclusionsOur findings demonstrate that Gene III(R) EGT effectively enhances exercise performance and alleviates fatigue. The underlying mechanisms involve the mitigation of oxidative stress and inflammation, as well as the activation of the AMPK/PGC-1 signaling pathway to promote mitochondrial function and cellular protection. These results highlight the potential of Gene III(R) EGT as a nutritional supplement for combating exercise-induced fatigue.
