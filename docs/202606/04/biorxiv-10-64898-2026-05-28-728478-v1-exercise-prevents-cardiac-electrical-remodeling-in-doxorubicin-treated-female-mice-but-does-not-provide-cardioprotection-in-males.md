## 摘要
背景：阿霉素（DOX）引起性别特异性心脏毒性。代谢损伤是DOX治疗公认的心脏毒性效应，可导致其他有害影响，如活性氧增加、ATP减少、炎症等。我们假设通过运动维持心脏代谢可减轻DOX的心脏毒性。

方法：将15周龄的雄性及雌性C57BL/6J小鼠随机分为四组：1）对照组（久坐），2）EX组（运动，跑步机跑步），3）DOX组（阿霉素5 mg/kg/周，持续6周），4）EXDOX组（运动+阿霉素）。在6周方案期间，每隔一周进行超声心动图测量心脏机械功能。方案结束时，进行光学标测和Seahorse分析分别测量电生理和代谢。还进行了RNA测序、细胞因子阵列分析和透射电子显微镜，以确定DOX心脏毒性的性别特异性机制。

结果：DOX仅降低雄性小鼠的每搏输出量和左心室直径，运动未能预防DOX的这些效应。在雌性小鼠中，DOX延长动作电位时程（APD）并减慢传导速度（CV），重要的是，运动预防了DOX诱导的CV减慢。运动对雌性小鼠DOX的心脏保护作用与维持有氧代谢和减轻炎症相关，后者调节离子通道基因表达。具体而言，Cacna1c在DOX和EXDOX雌性小鼠中均增加，但在雄性中未增加，并与APD延长相关。有趣的是，尽管CV减慢，Gja1和Scn5a却增加。然而，Kcnj8的增加与代谢损伤共同可能导致膜超极化，从而引起CV减慢。

结论：DOX心脏毒性具有性别特异性。机械功能障碍在DOX处理的雄性中更常见，而致心律失常性电重构在DOX处理的雌性中更常见。DOX治疗期间的运动疗法未能预防雄性心脏中DOX诱导的机械功能障碍，但通过维持代谢和减轻炎症减轻了雌性中的电重构。

## Abstract
BackgroundDoxorubicin (DOX) causes sex-specific cardiotoxicity. Metabolic impairment is a well-established cardiotoxic effect of DOX treatment that can contribute to other detrimental effects such as increased reactive oxygen species, reduced ATP, inflammation etc. We hypothesized that preserving cardiac metabolism by exercise can attenuate DOX cardiotoxicity.

MethodsMale and female C57BL/6J mice at 15 weeks of age were randomly assigned to one of four groups, 1) Control (sedentary), 2) EX (exercised, treadmill running), 3) DOX (doxorubicin at 5 mg/kg/week for 6 weeks), and 4) EXDOX (exercise + doxorubicin). Echocardiography was performed every other week during the 6-week protocol to measure cardiac mechanical function. At the end of the protocol, optical mapping and seahorse analysis were performed to measure electrophysiology and metabolism, respectively. RNA sequencing, cytokine array assay and transmission electron microscopy were also performed to determine sex-specific mechanisms of DOX cardiotoxicity.

ResultsDOX reduced stroke volume and left ventricular diameter in males only and exercise did not prevent these effects of DOX. In female mice, DOX prolonged action potential duration (APD) and slowed conduction velocity (CV), and importantly, exercise prevented DOX-induced CV slowing. Exercise-induced cardioprotection against DOX in female mice was associated with preservation of aerobic metabolism and attenuation of inflammation which modulated ion channel gene expression. Specifically, Cacna1c was increased in both DOX and EXDOX females, but not in males and correlated with APD prolongation. Interestingly, despite CV slowing, Gja1 and Scn5a were increased. However, increased Kcnj8 along with metabolic impairment could cause membrane hyperpolarization and underlie CV slowing.

ConclusionsDOX cardiotoxicity is sex specific. Mechanical dysfunction is more prevalent in DOX-treated males while arrhythmogenic electrical remodeling is more prevalent in DOX-treated females. Exercise therapy during DOX did not prevent DOX induced mechanical dysfunction in male hearts but attenuated electrical remodeling in females by preserving metabolism and attenuating inflammation.
