Abstract 22 

Regular exercise induces adaptations in skeletal muscle and other organ systems to 23 

improve physical performance and overall health. Exercise results in phosphorylation of 24 

5’ AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK) at threonine 172 (T172) of the a2 subunit; 25 

however, the role of this activation in cellular and functional adaptations has not been 26 

elucidated. To this end, we subjected non-activatable Ampk 

a2(T172A ) knock-in (KI) adult 27 

mice and wild-type (WT) littermates to 4 weeks of voluntary wheel running (VWR). 28 

Exercise training led to significant improvements in endurance capacity, maximal oxygen 29 

consumption ( 𝑉 ̇ O2max), and glucose tolerance, as well as skeletal muscle IIb-to-IIa fiber 30 

type shift in both WT and KI mice. Contrastingly, VWR resulted in increased mitochondrial 31 

OxPhos protein expression, mitochondrial volume density, and capillary density in skeletal 32 

muscle of WT but not KI mice. Exercise-induced improvements of mitochondrial 33 

respiration and conductance revealed by high-resolution respirometry of isolated 34 

mitochondria were blunted in KI mice. Therefore, for the first time, we reveal that AMPK a235 

T172 activation is required for exercise training-induced mitochondrial biogenesis, 36 

improvement of mitochondrial respiratory function, and angiogenesis in skeletal muscle, 37 

but that these adaptations are not solely responsible for improved 𝑉 ̇ O2max and exercise 38 

endurance capacity. 39

Introduction 52 

A fundamental theory of exercise physiology is that the human body adapts to the acute 53 

and chronic stress of physical activity to improve its maximal functional capacity (1). 54 

Exercise training, particularly endurance exercise, leads to adaptations in most systems, 55 

including the cardiovascular and musculoskeletal systems that support an overall increase 56 

in exercise capacity and performance (2, 3). Important clinically, exercise capacity, or 57 

cardiorespiratory fitness, is inversely associated with all-cause of mortality (4). Exercise 58 

training is now considered the most potent lifestyle intervention for health promotion and 59 

chronic disease prevention (5-7). 60 

Extensive research has been directed toward skeletal muscle adaptations responsible for 61 

improved exercise capacity and metabolic homeostasis. In a seminal study, John Holloszy 62 

(1967) demonstrated that in rodents, long-term treadmill running can double mitochondrial 63 

enzyme activities in skeletal muscle, providing the first biochemical evidence of exercise-64 

induced mitochondrial adaptation (8). The results of increased skeletal muscle 65 

mitochondrial content and electron transport chain enzymatic activity have since been 66 

reaffirmed in a vast number of studies (2, 9-11), thus laying the foundation for the idea 67 

that mitochondrial expansion is crucial for improved exercise endurance capacity after 68 

training (12-14). In another seminal study at the same time period, Carrow et al. (1967) 69 

showed that long-term treadmill running in rats increased capillary density in skeletal 70 

muscle (15), which has since been reaffirmed (16, 17). Intriguingly, these two lines of 71 

concurrent research focused on cellular structures that are responsible for the delivery 72 

and consumption of oxygen as energy system essential for ATP production to main 73 

function of skeletal muscle. Since then, significant focus has been given to the mass of 74 

the mitochondrial oxidative energy system and capillary remodeling in skeletal muscle (18-75 

28). The findings have led to a long-standing concept that mitochondrial and microvascular 76

In the last three decades, numerous studies have focused on identification and elucidation 80 

of signaling regulators for mitochondrial biogenesis and angiogenesis in skeletal muscle 81 

in response to exercise training. 5’ AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK), an energy 82 

sensor and regulator of metabolism (29-34), emerged as a primary determinant of skeletal 83 

muscle adaptation. AMPK is readily activated by phosphorylation at threonine 172 (T172) 84 

of the dominant a2 catalytic subunit in response muscle contraction and exercise (35, 36). 85 

In support of this conclusion, muscle-specific overexpression of a dominant-negative form 86 

of Ampk a2 resulted in diminished exercise-induced increases in mitochondrial content 87 

and mitochondria-specific antioxidant capacity (37). Muscle-specific deletion of a AMPK 88 

downstream target gene, peroxisome-proliferator-activated receptor g coactivator 1 a89 

(Pgc-1 

a) (38), blocked exercise training-induced mitochondrial biogenesis and 90 

angiogenesis in skeletal muscle (39, 40); whereas muscle-specific overexpression of Pgc-91 

1a promoted mitochondrial biogenesis and angiogenesis in skeletal muscle (41, 42). 92 

Furthermore, skeletal muscle-specific deletion of the upstream AMPK kinase, Lkb1, 93 

blunted exercise training-induced mitochondrial biogenesis in skeletal muscle (43). In 94 

contrast, there are findings that do not support the inferred importance of AMPK in skeletal 95 

muscle adaptation. For example, mice with muscle-specific deletion of the Ampk 

a2 gene 96 

or the Ampk 

a1/Ampk 

a2 genes, or muscle-specific overexpression of an inactive Ampk a297 

showed normal enhancement of mitochondrial biogenesis after training (44-47). Hence, 98 

the definitive roles of AMPK activation in skeletal muscle adaptation to exercise training 99 

remains to be fully elucidated. 100 

The interpretations of some of these previous findings may have been influenced by the 101 

methodology of gene deletion or dominant-negative overexpression approaches, which 102 

can alter protein stoichiometry (i.e., loss or overexpression of specific proteins) that have 103 

been shown to cause compensatory changes (48-50). Furthermore, prior works using 104 

these genetic models with exercise training have not comprehensively measured 105 

mitochondrial respiratory capacity, metabolic flexibility and free radical generation, which 106

To evaluate the role of AMPK a2 T172 activation in skeletal muscle adaptations in 109

capacity and VO 2max. For this reason, we comprehensively examined the role of AMPK a2115 

T172 activation in skeletal muscle mitochondrial adaptation at protein, structural and 116

performance. 119

To investigate the role of AMPK a2 T172 activation in skeletal muscle mitochondrial 212 

bioenergetics and redox regulation following exercise training, we performed a high-213 

resolution respirometry on mitochondria isolated from plantaris muscle using a creatine 214 

kinase clamp (Fig. 4A-B). Endurance training resulted in an increase of maximal 215 

respiration and enhanced respiratory conductance in WT mice, both of which were absent 216 

in KI mice (Fig. 4 C-F). In contrast, KI mice exhibited significantly elevated mitochondrial 217

Discussion 224 

Repeated bouts of exercise activate multiple signaling networks in skeletal muscle to 225 

induce adaptive changes that improve functional performance (60, 61). Although AMPK 226 

has been shown to be a central driving factor in endurance exercise-induced mitochondrial 227 

and metabolic adaptations in skeletal muscle (34, 47, 51, 62-65), much of the evidence 228 

derives from mouse genetic models wherein protein stoichiometry was disrupted (44-47). 229 

Using a CRISPR-Cas9-mediated gene editing targeting the Ampk a2 T172 230 

phosphorylation site, we previously demonstrated its essential role in overall energy 231 

metabolism at baseline and during exercise (51). In the present study, we further 232 

examined the role AMPK a2 T172 in exercise training-induced adaptations. Our data 233 

revealed that AMPK a2 T172 phosphorylation is required for exercise training-induced 234 

increases in mitochondrial content and respiratory function, as well as angiogenesis in 235

There are various instances of experimental evidence supporting our findings regarding 243 

the role of AMPK in mitochondrial adaptation in response to exercise. First, acute treadmill 244 

running-induced mRNA expression of mitochondrial and oxidative genes was attenuated 245 

in muscle-specific Ampk 

a1/2 knockout (KO) mice (44). Furthermore, long-term VWR 246 

promoted mitochondrial respiratory and Sod2 proteins in WT mice but not in muscle-247 

specific Ampk 

a2 kinase dead (KD) mice (37). Here, we used a new model system and 248 

obtained comprehensive evidence by expanding our measurements to mitochondrial 249 

ultra-structure and respiratory function as well as reactive oxygen species generation. Our 250 

findings support the notion that AMPK a2 T172 activation is required for endurance 251 

training-induced mitochondrial adaptation in skeletal muscle. However, studies using 252 

genetically modified mice, including skeletal muscle-specific knockout of Ampk a253 

subunit(s) or overexpression of an inactive Ampk 

a2 (DN), reported little impact of the loss 254 

of AMPK on exercise training-induced increases of mitochondrial proteins (44, 45, 47). 255 

Those discrepancies may reflect altered signaling landscape due to the disruptive nature 256 

of those genetic models. In this context, our KI model provides a distinct advantage by 257 

preserving the stoichiometry of AMPK while selectively abolishing canonical activation at 258 

AMPK a2 T172. 259

277 

Considering present and previous results, a remaining question concerns the mechanism 278 

by which AMPK activation may trigger adaptive processes in skeletal muscles following 279 

each bout of endurance exercise. AMPK has been shown to directly phosphorylate PGC-280 

1a (38), a master regulator of mitochondrial biogenesis and angiogenesis in skeletal 281 

muscle (69-71), and PGC-1 a’s nuclear/mitochondrial translocation and subsequent 282 

transcriptional activities have been shown to be dependent on AMPK (72-74). Importantly, 283 

exercise induced-mitochondrial biogenesis and angiogenesis, but not fiber-type 284 

transformation, in skeletal muscle have been shown to be dependent on Pgc-1 a (39, 40). 285 

It is important to note that our findings in the current study phenocopied these previous 286 

studies, supporting that AMPK activation in concert with PGC-1 a in inducing mitochondrial 287 

biogenesis and angiogenesis, but not to be responsible for fiber type transformation in 288 

skeletal muscle in response to endurance training. 289 

290 

Increased mitochondrial content and respiratory enzyme activity and improved capillary 291 

density in skeletal muscle are classically linked to functional improvements by exercise 292 

training (2, 8, 14, 17, 19, 75). These adaptations may reduce metabolic perturbation during 293 

exercise with enhanced fat oxidation and lactate clearance, leading to an improved 294 

intramyocellular homeostasis and, thereby, endurance exercise capacity (12, 18). 295 

Consistently, a recent study by García-Domínguez et al. reported that mitochondrial 296 

adaptations are functionally required for exercise-induced improvements in exercise 297 

capacity (14). An intriguing and unexpected finding of the present study is that exercise 298 

training-induced mitochondrial and angiogenic adaptations were ameliorated in KI mice, 299 

yet endurance exercise capacity and substrate selection during VO 2max testing were 300

310 

Over the years there have been suggestions that central factors, such as cardiac output, 311 

pulmonary system and blood flow, are main determining factor for 𝑉 ̇ O2max and endurance 312 

capacity in healthy, untrained adult subjects (13, 79, 80), whereas peripheral factors, such 313 

as mitochondrial mass in skeletal muscle, may become limiting during endurance 314 

exercise, aging and disease states (81, 82). Alternatively, classic and current findings may 315 

be interpreted to mean that our bodies are elegantly built with multiple redundancies in 316 

the regulation of carbon and oxygen flows, and their matching, under diverse 317 

circumstances. Thus, as in the present investigation, alteration of a single gene may 318 

produce ambiguous results when obviously multiple enzyme and organ systems are 319 

involved in controlling exercise endurance under diverse circumstances 320 

321 

Conclusion 322 

Phosphorylation of AMPK a2 T172 is a critical regulatory step in the structural and 323 

bioenergetic remodeling processes in skeletal muscle in response to endurance training 324 

in young, healthy animals. However, these adaptations are not obligatorily required for the 325 

improvement of exercise capacity following training. These findings reinforce the 326 

complexity and elegant redundancy of whole-body functional adaptation to exercise 327 

training. 328

High-resolution respirometry 431 

High resolution respirometry was used to assess mitochondrial respiratory rate ( JO2) or 432 

oxygen consumption rate (OCR) through Oroboros O2k (Innsbruck, Austria). The creatine 433

plantaris muscle. Compared to conventional ADP derived approach, the CK technique 435 

takes advantage of enzymatic reaction of CK to clamp ATP:ADP under a physiologically 436 

relevant range (Fig. 4 A and B)(88, 89). Through this platform, we were able to also 437 

measure the metabolic flexibility or mitochondrial conductance by having sequential 438 

phosphocreatine (PCr) titrations to create different levels of ATP free energies ( DGATP ). 439 

Theoretically, JO2decreases when DGATP decreases and vice versa, when electron 440 

transport chain (ETC) is tightly coupled to the changing demand for ATP re-synthesis. On 441 

days of the experiment, Oroboros O2k chambers were warmed to 37ºC and set to 500 442

air saturation, followed by saturating sodium dithionite (Sigma S1256) to record zero-point 445 

calibration. Following calibration, chambers were washed thoroughly with water, then 446 

refilled with Buffer D supplemented with 5mM creatine monohydrate (Sigma C0780). 10ug 447 

of mitochondria based on the Braford assay of each sample was added into each 448

Measurement of mitochondrial ROS emission 462 

Mitochondrial reactive oxygen species (ROS) emission was determined using Amplex 463 

Ultra Red (AUR), based on the concept that horseradish peroxidase (HRP) catalyzes the 464 

Hydrogen peroxide (H 2O2)-dependent oxidation of non-fluorescent Amplex Red to 465 

fluorescent resorufin. Superoxide dismutase was added to the reaction mixture to ensure 466 

that all superoxide is converted to H 2O2. Briefly, a standard curve for H 2O2 (25-250 nM) 467 

was prepared using buffer D, containing AUR stock solution (0.02 mM; prepared according 468 

to the manufacturer’s instructions), HRP (1U/mL), SOD (20 U/mL), auranofin (AF; 0.0001 469 

mM), malate (2.5 mM), pyruvate (5 mM), creatine kinase (20 mM), phosphocreatine (PCr; 470 

1mM), and ATP (5 mM) in a final volume of 0.6 mL. Fluorescence measurements were 471 

performed using a Horiba Quantmaster 4 Fluorometer (Edison, NJ, USA) under the 472 

following conditions: 37 °C, excitation/emission wavelengths of 565/600 nm, stirring at 500 473 

rpm, and a 0.875 mL cuvette with an adaptor. Data collection set to 1-second intervals 474 

with a 60-second pause, over a 4-minute period for each stage. The H 2O2 concentration 475 

in the reaction will range from 25 to 250 nM, using a 0.1 mM H 2O2 stock solution. The 476 

background fluorescence was subtracted using FelixFL software after 10 seconds of 477 

exposure prior to AUR addition with an open window. Further background correction was 478 

applied to each run based on the fluorescence rate before H 2O2 addition. The H 2O2479 

concentration was calculated as previously described range (88, 89). Following 480 

mitochondrial isolation from the plantaris and protein quantification (described above), 10 481 

μg of mitochondria each sample was added to the pre-warmed cuvette under the same 482 

conditions as the O2k assay. Electron leak was expressed as a percentage of total flux, 483
